About

Our Esteemed Museum

Museo Iloilo started from a concept of having a public repository of cultural artifacts in Iloilo almost 50 years ago. However, it was then Tourism Commissioner Gregorio Araneta II of the Board of Travel and Tourist Industry (BTTI) who made this concept realized. With BTTI Regional Director Rodolfo Claporols who solicited government funds and Iloilo Provincial Governor Conrado Norado who donated the 2,000 square meters lot where Museo Iloilo stands today, Araneta ably managed to come up with a floating rate sum of ₱160,000.00 for the building and ₱115,000.00 for exhibits, display system, and operating expenses.

Our Building

A beautiful and modern building, Museo Iloilo was designated by an Ilonggo Architect Segio Peñasales, with Jorge Tamayo as the Structural Engineer, Oliver Jardiolin as the Electrical Engineer, and Rogelio Peñasales as the Associate Consultant. It opened its doors to the public on May 28, 1971. The nine different sections of Museo Iloilo showcase Western Visayas Heritage, from the artifacts of a shipwreck discovered near Guimaras Island, some antiques from ancestral heirlooms of the Dellotas, Jesenas and Consings to a rich collection that ranges from fossilized stones, shells, tooth, pre-colonial pottery, gold mask, wooden and ivory saints, old and rare Iloilo pictures, manuscripts, jusi and piña cloth, and memorabilia of famous Ilonggos. It supports local artists through monthly exhibits of their artworks; and encourages and develops new generation of artists through On-the-spot Painting Contests and Summer Art Workshops.

Our Exhibit

Museo Iloilo has a hall of permanent exhibits, art gallery of changing exhibits, and an archive on Western Visayan culture, history, and art. Like museums all over the world, it charges nominal admission fees and provides tour guides when needed.

Since 1973 up to present, Museo Iloilo is under the ownership and management of Iloilo Cultural Research Foundation, Inc. headed by Mrs. Gina Agnes Espinosa as President, Chona Bondoc as Vice President, Atty. Lina Layson as Secretary, Mrs. Imelda Florete as Treasurer, and Sergio Peñasales, Mrs. Ida Endonila, Mr. James Mozart Amsua, Mr. Edwin Bautista, Mrs. Antonnietta Chan and Mrs. Narzalina Lim as Members. The museum staff includes Mrs. Maura Gellangarin – Admin Assistant, Janry Tanalgo – Staff, and Mrs. Emiliana Blaseco.

What makes Museo Iloilo (MI) unique from the rest of the local museums in the city is that MI, has two art galleries, its encourages to showcased local art works. The Museo Iloilo produced local visual artists that they are recognized by art governing bodies like Phil Art Awards, Metrobank, Shell Pilipinas, Petron, and other artist's residency insitute here and abroad.
