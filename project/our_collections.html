Collections

Mueseo Iloilo has nine different sections. Gallery I and Gallery II are where paintings for sale and on exhibit are displayed.

The Archeological Section includes jars and wares found in different places in Iloilo. There is a coffin made of woods, mostly found in caves, used for burial rituals; a burial vessel made of limestone in which bones from the coffin are transferred to after five years; a skeleton with gold mask which was excavated in San Antonio, Oton (the gold mask is just a replica for viewing since the original gold mask is at the National Museum in Manila); Chinese Celadon Plates found in San Dionisio, Iloilo which is used to serve foods for the king and Queen; Manunggul Jars used for food and water storage found in Isla Gigantes in Carles; and Mother, Father and child plate which are China wares made during the Sung Dyansty.

Archeological Section
Textile & Weaving

The Weaving Section features the replica of the weaving looms used to make Hablon and Patadyong. It symbolizes wealth because people during those time who have it in their houses means they belong to the rich family. The section also presents the finished products made of piña, abaca and jusi.

In the World War II Section, weapons used by the Japanese Army such as riffles, bombs, helmet, missile, and samurai can be found. This is also where the radio transmitters used by Ilonggos to communicate to Gen. McArthur and the US Navy, and the Mickey Mouse Monies, which is currently of no value, donated by rich families in Jaro are located. A letter that saved the Iloilo City from bombing; the battle trophy of Lt. Hugo Chavez in the revolution against Spain donated by the family of his son, the late Don Jose Chavez of Jordan, Guimaras; and the sword of Gen. Perfecto Poblador and the uniform of Epifanio Concepcion who are allies of Gen. Martin Delgado during Japanese war are also displayed in this section.

The Religious Section is composed of Santo Intierro, the dead Christ in a glass coffin; the wooden statue of St. Vincent Ferrer holding a child with the Jaro belfry picture which is the only belfry that is separated from its church; the picture of the Molo Church belfry; a hundred year old wooden sculpture of Saint Joseph; the crucified Christ oF IBRI which means leus Narazeius Rex leudrorium; the wooden statue of San Roque, a saint who petitioned for deliverance from sickness and pestilence; late 18th century high relief carving in three pieces of hardwoord, originally polychrome showing traces of gesso; other saints donated to Meseo Iloilo; and some saints made of elephant tusk.

The Cultural Communities Section, the things that the Negritos used before can be found such as the mortar and pestle used to pound rice, the horn that the father used to call the attention of his child, salamander skin used for clothing of babies, fishnet used for catching fish, and the replica of a child Ati with a bamboo guitar in his lap.
